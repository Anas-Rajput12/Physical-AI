# AI-Native Software Development Academic Book Constitution

## Core Principles

### I. Verifiable Accuracy and Sourcing
All content must be based on verifiable primary sources. Every factual claim must be supported by an APA 7th-edition citation. A minimum of 50% of all sources must be from peer-reviewed research, and each chapter must cite a minimum of 15 sources.

### II. Academic Rigor and Clarity
Writing must be academically clear and targeted at a graduate-level computer-science audience. All content is required to have zero plagiarism. The text must adhere to a Flesch-Kincaid grade level of 10–12, and all technical terms must be clearly defined upon their first use.

### III. Standardized Chapter Structure
Every chapter must include an abstract, an introduction, a well-structured body with diagrams and illustrations as needed, a conclusion, and a comprehensive list of references.

### IV. Translation Readiness
The text must be authored to be suitable for translation. This requires avoiding idioms, culturally specific expressions, and other non-translatable phrases. All diagrams, including their textual elements, must be designed to be easily translatable.

### V. Technical and Formatting Requirements
Each chapter must be between 5,000 and 7,000 words. The final output must be exportable to PDF with embedded citations and must also be Markdown-friendly to ensure compatibility with Docusaurus. All content must undergo a rigorous fact-checking and plagiarism-detection process before finalization.

## Governance
All contributions and content must strictly adhere to the principles outlined in this constitution. Any proposal to amend this document requires a formal review and approval process. The reasoning for any changes must be documented in an Architectural Decision Record (ADR).
