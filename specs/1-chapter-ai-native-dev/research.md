# Research: Chapter on AI-Native Software Development

**Purpose**: To define the chapter's structure and outline the key topics for literature review and content creation.

## Decision: Chapter Outline

The chapter will be structured as a formal academic paper with the following sections to ensure a logical and pedagogical flow.

1.  **Abstract**: A concise summary of the chapter's content, including the core definition, key principles, and main conclusions.
2.  **Introduction**: Provides context, defines the scope, and presents the central thesis. It will explain why AI-native software development is a critical new paradigm.
3.  **The Definition of AI-Native**: A dedicated section to establish a clear, well-sourced definition of the term, distinguishing it from related concepts.
4.  **Historical Evolution**: Traces the origins from earlier concepts (e.g., expert systems, DevOps, MLOps) to the current understanding.
5.  **Key Principles**: A detailed exploration of the core tenets, such as:
    *   AI as a core component, not an add-on.
    *   Continuous learning and adaptation in production systems.
    *   Automation of development and operational workflows via AI.
    *   The role of autonomous agents.
6.  **Architecture Patterns**: Discussion of common architectural styles, including agent-based systems, LLM-centric designs, and data-driven feedback loops.
7.  **Workflow Differences**: A comparative analysis of the AI-native development lifecycle versus traditional software development (e.g., SDLC, Agile), highlighting changes in roles, processes, and tools.
8.  **Major AI Models Involved**: An overview of the types of AI models that underpin this paradigm (e.g., Large Language Models, Reinforcement Learning models, Foundational Models).
9.  **Practical Applications & Case Studies**: Real-world examples of AI-native systems to illustrate the concepts in practice.
10. **Risks and Challenges**: A balanced discussion of potential downsides, including ethical considerations, model reliability, and new security vectors.
11. **Conclusion**: Summarizes the key findings and speculates on the future trajectory of AI-native software development.
12. **References**: A comprehensive list of all sources cited, formatted in APA 7th Edition.

## Rationale

This structure was chosen because it aligns with academic standards for review articles and textbook chapters. It begins with foundational definitions, builds on them with principles and patterns, provides practical context through applications, and concludes with a forward-looking perspective. This linear approach is optimal for readers new to the concept.

## Alternatives Considered

A thematic approach was considered, with sections organized around concepts like "Automation," "Autonomy," and "Adaptation." However, this was rejected as it could be less clear for the target audience and might lead to repetition. The chosen linear structure provides a more straightforward narrative.
